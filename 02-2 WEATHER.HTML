Pets more irritable during intense heat, says San Lazaro Hospital, as it records surge in animal bite cases

 
From ABS-CBN News
Published May 08, 2024 07:39 PM PHT
 
 
 
MANILA -- The San Lazaro Hospital in Manila has observed that animals tend to be more irritable during the intense heat, as the hospital recorded a surge in animal bite cases this month.

Dr. David Suplico, San Lazaro Hospital's chief medical professional staff, said Wednesday that it is also apparent that people have become more aware of rabies and its implications.

"Tumaas po ang awareness within Metro Manila kaya nakikita namin isa rin po siyang dahilan kung bakit kami dinadagsa po ng pasyente ngayon," he added.

(Rabies awareness grew within Metro Manila, which we think is one of the reasons why hordes of patients are coming here.)

With this, Suplico said the hospital is experiencing an "unusual surge" in animal bite cases.

"Mainit po iyong panahon. 'Pag mainit po iyong panahon, nakikita po natin na ang mga bata po lumalabas sa kanilang bahay at naglalaro. 'Pag naglaro po sila, pati po ang mga hayop natin medyo aligaga 'pag tag-init, na konting hawak lang, kung maapakan naiirita po sila at nangangagat," he said.

(The weather is hot -- that's when we see many children go outside their homes and play. When they play, our animals are also rather restless amid the heat that when they are touched or stepped on, they get irritated and bite.)

From the 1,300 daily animal bite patients in April, they have been dealing with 3,300 daily animal bite patients since the start of May. The hospital recorded 33,133 total consultations on animal bites from April 1 to 30, while there are already 14,088 total consultations from May 1 to 7, equivalent to almost half of the whole month of April.

"Tinatanggap po namin lahat ng mga pasyente dito po sa San Lazaro Hospital. Pero humihingi po ako ng pang-unawa sa mga pasyente po... meron po kaming mga mainit na lugar, lahat po ng mga consequence ng mga mahabang pila. Humihingi po ako ng pasensya po, pang-unawa po sa kanila," Suplico explained.

He encouraged patients especially from far places to check Animal Bite Treatment Centers in their localities.

"Doon sana po sila pupunta para po magpatingin (They can go there to get checked up)," he added. The hospital is also adding manpower to tend to patients, but Suplico said it has affected their other operations.
