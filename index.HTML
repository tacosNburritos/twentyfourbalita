Extreme heat situations can impact animal health as well as human health. Animals can overheat, suffer dehydration, and even die in hot weather. Protect your animals during extreme heat situations.

Temperatures above 85°F are too hot and below 32°F are too cold to keep your dog outside for long. You can take precautions to keep your pooch safe, like checking hot pavement with the back of your hand or wiping snowy paws with a damp rag. Extreme hot and cold weather can be dangerous to your dog.

Move the dog to a shaded and cool area. Immediately pour cool (not cold to avoid shock) water over the dog. Tap water (15-16°C) has been found to be the most effective at cooling dogs with heat-related illnesses. In a true emergency, any water is better than nothing.
